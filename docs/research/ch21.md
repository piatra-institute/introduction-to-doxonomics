## 2. Recent scholarship the chapter should engage

### 2.1 Updated experimental evidence on cooperation

- **Fosgaard 2023 / Cambridge Core 2023** "What drives conditional cooperation in public good games?" *Experimental Economics* 26(2). Finds that residual factors (confusion, anchoring) explain ~40% of apparent conditional-cooperative behavior; the rest is inequality aversion, some conformity, very little reciprocity. This refines Fischbacher 2001 rather than undercuts it. [`fosgaard2023` — verify volume]
- **Thöni & Volk 2018** "Conditional cooperation: Review and refinement" *Economics Letters* (verify) — updated typology across dozens of experiments. [`thoni2018` — verify]
- **Girardi 2024** "Does studying economics make you selfish?" *Southern Economic Journal*. Diff-in-diff identification strategy; updates Frank-Gilovich-Regan. [`girardi2024` — verify SEJ venue and volume]

### 2.2 Updated neoliberal-institutional-history scholarship

- **Mirowski 2023** "The Neoliberal Thought Collective Under Erasure." INET Working Paper. [`mirowski2023`]
- **Slobodian 2023** *Crack-Up Capitalism*. Metropolitan. [`slobodian2023`]
- **Mayer 2016** (already in bib as `mayer2016`) — no newer book-length update; her journalistic follow-ups in *The New Yorker* remain the primary venue.

### 2.3 Crowding-out and motivation literature

- **Gneezy & Rustichini 2000** "A Fine is a Price" *Journal of Legal Studies* (verify). The canonical daycare-fine result. [`gneezy2000` — verify venue]

## 6. Open questions specific to Ch. 21

1. **Parameter calibration for the regime-competition model** (Model 21.2). The illustrative parameters give a $260M/year counter-investment threshold. Empirical calibration is an open question; the book should either flag this as Ch. 21 exercise material (it partially does) or cite the candidate methods explicitly.
2. **Meta-analysis of the FGR effect.** With Carter-Irons 1991, Frank-Gilovich-Regan 1993, Bauman-Rose 1996, and Girardi 2024, the literature is ripe for synthesis. No book-level meta-analysis exists.
3. **Cross-cultural replication of the conditional-cooperator finding.** Fischbacher 2001 is Swiss; Henrich 2001 covers 15 small-scale societies; the 2023 Fosgaard paper is German / Northern European. A WEIRD/non-WEIRD comparison on the PGG typology is an open empirical project.
4. **Historical audit of the Mont Pèlerin / Atlas institutional investment.** Mirowski 2009 and Slobodian 2018 cover some of this; a dollar-weighted time-series across 1947–2025 does not exist publicly.
