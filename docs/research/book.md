# Book-Level Research Dossier

**Status:** Initial draft, 2026-04-18. First pass. To be extended.
**Scope:** Intellectual landscape surrounding *Introduction to Doxonomics*: neighboring fields, recent scholarship (post-2018, especially post-2022), non-English traditions, and candidate additions to `bibliography/references.bib`.
**Companion:** per-chapter dossiers in this directory (starting with `ch01.md`).

The book's thesis is that material power shapes public belief through measurable institutional infrastructure. This dossier maps the fields doing adjacent work, the recent contributions the manuscript should engage, and the non-Anglo-Saxon traditions that risk being left as terrain rather than origin.


## Source-tier conventions

Following `docs/research-pipeline.md` (as adapted from CYK):

- **T1 — primary**: charter texts, statutes, court decisions, annual reports, government inquiries, original data releases.
- **T2 — scholarly**: peer-reviewed journal articles and university-press monographs.
- **T3 — authoritative reference**: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Oxford DNB, university-hosted encyclopedias.
- **T4 — secondary / journalism**: quality press, magazine essays. Useful for leads and contemporary figures; never the sole support for a structural claim.

Each proposed addition is tagged with tier, confidence (`[HIGH]`/`[MED]`/`[LOW]` for the match between the source and the book's use of it), and priority (`[P1]` load-bearing, `[P2]` strengthening, `[P3]` optional).


## 1. Foundational traditions the book inherits from

These are the deep roots. The book already cites the canonical entries. Notes below flag where a richer entry-point might be added.

### 1.1 Sociology of knowledge

- Mannheim 1936, Berger & Luckmann 1966 (already in bib).
- **Add**: Swidler & Arditi (1994) "The new sociology of knowledge" *Annual Review of Sociology*. A widely cited review that positions the tradition against earlier Mannheimian and Bergerian forms and connects it to Bourdieusian cultural-capital work. [T2, HIGH, P2]
- Recent: *Rethinking Knowledge Production in Sociology*, Cambridge Core 2024. Conceptual and institutional critique of how the discipline produces knowledge. [T2, MED, P3]

### 1.2 Symbolic power / Bourdieu

- Bourdieu 1984, 1991 (already in bib).
- **Add**: Bourdieu (1977) *Outline of a Theory of Practice*, for the primary formulation of *doxa*. [T1-for-Bourdieu, HIGH, P1]
- Recent empirical applications to add as demonstration of the framework's ongoing productivity:
  - Lindell (2022) "Symbolic Violence and the Social Space: Self-imposing the Mark of Disgrace?" *Cultural Sociology*. Multiple correspondence analysis on Swedish survey (n = 2,003), ties media practice to capital volume and composition. [T2, HIGH, P2]
  - Ingram (2024) critical application of Bourdieu to academically selective English education, *Critical Studies in Education*. [T2, MED, P2]
  - Studsrød et al. (2024) on Bourdieu and Norwegian welfare state, *Nordic Social Work Research*. [T2, MED, P3]
  - Giarelli (2025) "Symbolic cryptoviolence in online social networks" *Ethics and Information Technology*. Platform-era extension of symbolic violence. [T2, HIGH, P2]

### 1.3 Hegemony / Gramsci

- Gramsci 1971 (already in bib).
- **Add**: Liguori (2022, English translation) *Gramsci Contested: Interpretations, Debates, and Polemics, 1922–2012* (Brill). The best recent map of the internal scholarly debate in Italian and international Gramsci studies. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- **Add**: Martin (2022) *Hegemony* (Key Concepts in Political Theory). Newell Polity's concise theoretical primer. [T2, HIGH, P2]
- Recent empirical application: May, Nölke, Schedelik (2025) "Growth models and social blocs: Taking Gramsci seriously" *Competition & Change*. Applies Gramscian hegemony to comparative political economy. [T2, MED, P3]

### 1.4 Propaganda model and its empirical life

- Herman & Chomsky 1988 (already in bib), Lippmann 1922, Bernays 1928, Ellul 1965 (already in bib).
- **Add**: Pedro-Carañana, Broudy & Klaehn (eds, 2018) *The Propaganda Model Today: Filtering Perception and Awareness* (University of Westminster Press, open access). The definitive modern empirical compendium; contains the empirical corroborations the book's §"The doxonomic research program" should cite when claiming the PM is testable. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- Recent empirical tests to reference:
  - Applications to Ecuador 2019 protests (Springer 2021 chapter).
  - 2024 quantitative content analysis of CNN / BBC / RT / CGTN / Al Jazeera / TRT World / Times of Israel (venue verification needed).
  - Comparative study of media/political discourses around Russia-Ukraine war 2022–2024 across five countries (venue verification needed).

### 1.5 Agnotology / the production of ignorance

- Proctor & Schiebinger (2008) *Agnotology: The Making and Unmaking of Ignorance* (Stanford UP). Not currently in bib; should be added. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- **Add (forthcoming)**: Proctor & Schiebinger (2025, September) *Unmasked: Essays in the New Science of Agnotology* (Stanford UP, due). Second edited volume; will update the agnotology canon as the book goes to final. Mark as forthcoming, add on publication. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- **Add**: Oreskes & Conway (2010) *Merchants of Doubt* — already in bib as `oreskes2010`. No update needed.

### 1.6 Neoliberal thought collective

- Mirowski 2009, Mayer 2016, Slobodian 2018 (already in bib).
- **Add**: Mirowski (2023) "The Neoliberal Thought Collective Under Erasure" Institute for New Economic Thinking Working Paper. Mirowski's own most recent statement; directly updates the 2009 diagnosis. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- **Add**: Slobodian (2023) *Crack-Up Capitalism: Market Radicals and the Dream of a World Without Democracy* (Metropolitan / Allen Lane). The Zones / SEZ turn of neoliberalism; structurally the 2020s' update to *Globalists*. [T2, HIGH, P1]

### 1.7 Attention economy / surveillance capitalism

- Zuboff (2019) *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism* — already in bib as `zuboff2019`.
- **Add**: Curran (2023) "Surveillance capitalism and systemic digital risk" *Big Data & Society* 10(2). A prominent empirical-theoretical pushback on Zuboff's strong prediction claims; includes the imperative-to-collect argument. [T2, HIGH, P1]

## 2. Recent scholarship to engage (2020–2026)

These are post-writing-freeze works. They do not always demand citation, but a book that frames itself as *toward* a science should acknowledge the adjacent literature the field has produced since the initial draft.

### 2.1 Computational and platform-era propaganda

- Woolley & Howard (2019) *Computational Propaganda: Political Parties, Politicians, and Political Manipulation on Social Media* (Oxford UP). Nine-country empirical base; the template for post-2016 empirical study of social-media-mediated belief production. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- Howard, Lin, Tuzov (2023) "Computational propaganda: Concepts, methods, and challenges" *Global Media and China* (SAGE). [T2, HIGH, P2]
- 2025 extension: "The Limits of Computational Propaganda: Investigating Underexplored Platforms and Contexts" *Political Communication*. Engages the non-WEIRD / non-Western cases where the computational-propaganda frame has been understudied. [T2, MED, P2]

### 2.2 Network propaganda / asymmetric media ecosystems

- Benkler, Faris, Roberts (2018) *Network Propaganda: Manipulation, Disinformation, and Radicalization in American Politics* (Oxford UP). The empirical map of the US right-wing media ecosystem 2015–2018. **Already in bib as `benkler2018`.** [T2, HIGH, already-present]
- Note: no 2023–2024 book-scale follow-up by this team found in first-pass search; the scholarly descendants are journal articles on specific disinformation events.

### 2.3 Misinformation, inoculation, and correction (cognitive layer)

- Lewandowsky, Ecker, Seifert, Schwarz & Cook (2012) "Misinformation and Its Correction: Continued Influence and Successful Debiasing" *Psychological Science in the Public Interest* 13(3). The foundational modern review. [T2, HIGH, P2]
- Chan et al. (2023) "A meta-analysis of correction effects in science-relevant misinformation." [T2, HIGH, P2]
- Simchon, Zipori, Teitelbaum, Lewandowsky & van der Linden (2025) Signal-Detection-Theory meta-analysis of 33 inoculation experiments (N = 37,075). *Current Opinion in Psychology* (verify). [T2, HIGH, P2]
- Lewandowsky & van der Linden (2021-onward) on pre-emptive inoculation. Good for the book's Ch. 32–33 counter-strategy material.

### 2.4 Disinformation as collaborative work

- Starbird, Arif & Wilson (2019) "Disinformation as Collaborative Work: Surfacing the Participatory Nature of Strategic Information Operations" *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction* 3 (CSCW). Reframes disinformation from "single actor broadcasts lie" to networked participatory construction; directly relevant to the book's §"Internalization" and §"Circulation" stages. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- Center for an Informed Public (UW) — ongoing institutional research output 2020–present. T4 for institutional context; T2 for specific publications.

### 2.5 Dark-money and think-tank networks (quantitative)

- Walker et al. (forthcoming / 2024) Atlas-Network analyses around the Australian Voice referendum. Expands Mayer 2016 beyond the US. [T3–T2, MED, P2]
- SourceWatch / OpenSecrets operational data: Atlas Network 2023 revenue ≈ $28M, grants ≈ $7.6M to partners across ~500 orgs in ~100 countries. Useful for updating the book's think-tank-budget accounting. [T1 for OpenSecrets data; T3 for SourceWatch.]
- Review of Corporate Finance Studies (2024) "Shining a Light on Firms' Political Connections: The Role of Dark Money" 14(4):989. Corporate-finance perspective on dark-money flows; quantitative. [T2, MED, P2]

### 2.6 Henrich and the WEIRD critique

- Henrich (2020) *The WEIRDest People in the World: How the West Became Psychologically Peculiar and Particularly Prosperous* (FSG). [T2, HIGH, P1]
- Henrich, Heine & Norenzayan (2010) "The weirdest people in the world?" *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 33(2-3):61-83 — foundational. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- Critical responses: Grosjean on marriage-age counter-evidence; Guyatt in *LRB*; Brysbaert (2026) on Anglo-English standardization bias.

### 2.7 AI and epistemic authority (emerging)

- MDPI *Societies* (2025) "Artificial Truth: Algorithmic Power, Epistemic Authority, and the Crisis of Democratic Knowledge." Frames algorithmic recommendation as infrastructural epistemic governance; methodologically relevant to the book's §"Doxonomic power." [T2, HIGH, P2]
- *Taylor & Francis Social Epistemology* (2025) "AI and Epistemic Agency: How AI Influences Belief Revision and Its Normative Implications." [T2, MED, P3]


## 3. Non-English traditions

The book risks a standard Anglo-Saxon canon (Mannheim, Berger/Luckmann, Bourdieu read in English, Gramsci read in English). Each region below has a scholarly tradition on belief production that deserves acknowledgment.

### 3.1 French

- Bronner, Gérald *L'empire des croyances* (PUF, 2003) and subsequent work. A sociologist-of-beliefs research program with explicit cognitive-markets framing. Not currently in bib. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- Boudon, Raymond — the canonical French sociologist of rationality and belief. A citation to Boudon's *L'art de se persuader des idées douteuses, fragiles ou fausses* (1990) or similar would signal engagement with the French sociological tradition. [T2, HIGH, P2]
- Archives de sciences sociales des religions and L'Année sociologique issues on "sociologie de la croyance" (2010 special issue). [T3, MED, P3]
- Luc Boltanski (with Ève Chiapello) *Le nouvel esprit du capitalisme* (1999). A French-intellectual-history contribution on the coproduction of capitalist justification — highly relevant to the book's Ch. 22–23 (meritocracy, consumerism). [T2, HIGH, P1]

### 3.2 German

- Karl Mannheim's *Ideologie und Utopie* (1929) — already in bib as `mannheim1936` (English translation). A note that the German original predates the translation by seven years would sharpen the historical framing.
- Jürgen Habermas — *Strukturwandel der Öffentlichkeit* (1962), English as *The Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere* (1989). Adjacent to the book's concern with how institutions shape public discourse. **Already in bib as `habermas1962`.** Uncited in Ch. 1 despite its direct relevance; consider adding to §"Notes and References." [T2, HIGH, already-present]
- Max Horkheimer & Theodor Adorno *Dialektik der Aufklärung* (1944 ms, 1947 pub) — source for the culture-industry concept. `adorno1944` in bib. Confirmed.
- Wissenssoziologie tradition through Peter Ludes and others (post-2000); secondary relevance. [T2, LOW, P3]

### 3.3 Italian

- Gramsci, *Quaderni del carcere* (original 1929–1935 ms, pub 1948–1951 Einaudi). English Prison Notebooks (Buttigieg ed., 2007) is the scholarly edition. The book currently cites `gramsci1971` which is the Hoare & Nowell-Smith *Selections*; a complementary citation of the Buttigieg full Prison Notebooks would signal non-abridged engagement. [T1, HIGH, P2]
- Liguori (2022 translation, cited in §1.3 above) — the modern Italian-origin Gramsci scholarship.

### 3.4 Spanish / Portuguese / Latin America

- Paulo Freire, *Pedagogia do oprimido* (1968). The canonical Latin American contribution on how institutional pedagogy shapes belief and what counter-pedagogy looks like. Directly relevant to the book's Ch. 33 (epistemic democracy). **Already in bib as `freire1968`.** Check that it is cited where it belongs. [T1, HIGH, already-present]
- Aníbal Quijano — *colonialidad del poder* (1992 onwards). Latin American source for the race-and-coloniality chapter (Ch. 25). [T2, HIGH, P1]
- Boaventura de Sousa Santos — *Epistemologies of the South* (2014). Portuguese-origin argument about the exclusion of non-Western epistemic traditions from the global knowledge economy. Non-WEIRD epistemology. [T2, HIGH, P1]

### 3.5 South Asian / Indian

- Partha Chatterjee — *The Politics of the Governed* (2004), earlier *Nationalist Thought and the Colonial World* (1986). Subaltern studies' contribution to how colonial and post-colonial institutions produce the categories through which populations become legible. [T2, HIGH, P1]
- Dipesh Chakrabarty *Provincializing Europe* (2000). Argument against taking European intellectual history as default. [T2, HIGH, P2]
- Gayatri Spivak — 1988 essay "Can the Subaltern Speak?" on epistemic exclusion. [T1, HIGH, P1]

### 3.6 East Asian

- Historical/contemporary Japanese, Chinese, and Korean scholarship on mass media and public-opinion formation is comparatively underrepresented in the book's current bibliography (which `tools/doxo.py refs` confirms). A single strong citation per tradition would flag engagement:
  - Japanese media-studies tradition (post-Kagawa) on *seron* formation.
  - Chinese-language propaganda-studies scholarship (e.g., Anne-Marie Brady 2008 *Marketing Dictatorship*, English-language but on Chinese material; supplementing with Chinese-language scholarship is the open gap).
  - The Wind Information (万得信息) case study and similar — if the book touches platform-mediated financial / political information in China, these are the primary-source grounds.


## 4. Adjacent fields to engage

The book currently locates itself among: political economy, sociology of knowledge, media studies, propaganda studies, social epistemology, behavioral economics (Ch. 1 §"Neighboring fields" table). These additional adjacencies deserve at least a sentence's acknowledgment somewhere:

- **Science and Technology Studies (STS)**: Latour, Callon, Bloor's Strong Programme. The SSK (sociology of scientific knowledge) tradition is relevant because it has already built much of the apparatus for how collective belief about "what is known" gets materially stabilized.
- **Information science / classification theory**: Bowker & Star *Sorting Things Out* (1999) on classification as infrastructure. Directly relevant to Ch. 2 (ontology) and Ch. 9 (doxonomic power). Not currently in bib.
- **Computational social science**: Lazer et al. (2009) and follow-ups. Methodological ally for Ch. 13–17.
- **Cognitive linguistics**: Lakoff on political framing (*Moral Politics* 1996, *Don't Think of an Elephant* 2004). Directly relevant to Ch. 7 (narrative infrastructure). Not currently in bib.
- **Rhetoric and argumentation studies**: Perelman & Olbrechts-Tyteca *The New Rhetoric* (1958/1969). Pre-internet foundation for the study of persuasion-as-institutional-practice.
- **Network science / diffusion research**: Watts, Centola, Lazer. Methodological ally for Ch. 15.

## 6. Open empirical questions

The book already acknowledges that empirical calibration is an open research program. The following are the specific open problems this dossier surfaces that a future research round could pursue.

1. **A global audit of doxonomic expenditure.** The book assembles US figures for the selfishness regime (~$10–18B/year). A parallel audit for the EU, UK, Canada, India, Japan, Brazil does not exist. First-mover opportunity.
2. **A replication meta-analysis of the Frank-Gilovich-Regan effect.** Multiple follow-ups (Girardi 2024 most recent). No synthesis across the full 30-year literature. The book cites the effect in Ch. 21; a stronger synthesis would sharpen Prop. `prop:econ-education`.
3. **Institutional-funding network analysis of Atlas Network partners.** Atlas discloses ~500 orgs; no public network analysis of co-funding and personnel flows across this graph has been done at the scale the book's framework implies.
4. **Empirical tests of the belief production chain as a Markov process.** The book formalizes the chain as composition of operators; whether real transitions between stages (funding → processing → narrative → circulation → internalization → behavior → reproduction) are Markovian, semi-Markovian, or strongly path-dependent is not settled empirically.
5. **Regional tests of the propaganda-model filters outside the US/UK.** Pedro-Carañana et al. 2018 has case studies; the systematic cross-regional test (including non-Western cases) remains thin.
6. **Calibration of the first-order belief equation (eq. `eq:first-order`) to specific cases.** Currently illustrative; no empirical dataset of $(F, C, P, T, X)$ values for a specific belief has been assembled.


## 7. Research pipeline notes

This dossier was produced by web-search + web-fetch passes (2026-04-18). Known limitations of that first pass:

- **Paywalled sources** (Wiley, Oxford Academic, Elsevier) returned 403/paywall. Confirming specific citation metadata from these requires institutional access. Treat 2024-era journal references herein as "metadata-likely-correct; verify volume/issue/pages before adding to bib."
- **Non-English sources** were searched in English-language mode; the French and Italian results are surface-level. A second pass in each native language would surface substantially more. For Chinese and Arabic scholarship the first pass returned nothing of substance; this is a known gap.
- **Book-length follow-ups** surface well in search; journal-article follow-ups less so. The dossier is biased toward books.
- **No primary-source verification** has been done for any of the proposed additions yet. Each is a hypothesis to be confirmed when the bib entry is drafted.
