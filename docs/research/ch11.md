**Thesis:** Doxonomic dynamics can be captured by five model families — stock-and-flow, SIR/epidemiological, Bayesian updating, cascade, and competitive — each illuminating a different mechanism. All models are stylized; calibration is a major open problem.
**Companion:** `docs/research/book.md`.

Ch. 11 is the mathematical core. It carries a specific risk: any formal chapter in a field-founding book invites skepticism from two sides — applied researchers who want empirical calibration, and modelers who want rigor. The dossier prioritizes (a) recent foundational reviews the chapter should cite, and (b) methodological neighbors that would strengthen the claim that the five families are the right taxonomy.

## 2. Recent scholarship the chapter should engage

### 2.1 Opinion dynamics — the broader modeling family

The chapter's five-family taxonomy is solid but does not name **opinion dynamics** as its own tradition. The French-DeGroot-Hegselmann-Krause lineage is the closest neighbor to the chapter's stock-and-flow and Bayesian models.

- **DeGroot, M.H. 1974** "Reaching a consensus" *JASA* 69(345):118–121. The foundational averaging model. Not in bib. [`degroot1974`]
- **Hegselmann & Krause 2002** "Opinion dynamics and bounded confidence: models, analysis, and simulation" *JASSS* 5(3). Foundational bounded-confidence model. Canonical. [`hegselmann2002`]
- **Bernardo et al. 2023** "Bounded confidence opinion dynamics: A survey" *Automatica* (verify). Recent review. [`bernardo2023`]
- **Noorazar 2023** "Opinion Dynamics: A Comprehensive Overview" *arXiv* (verify year / publication). [`noorazar2023`]

Recommendation: a new short subsection in §"Model Comparison" that names opinion-dynamics models as a sixth family or acknowledges them as the continuous-state cousin of the binary SIR models. The chapter currently leaves a reader from control-theory or mathematical-sociology wondering why DeGroot / HK are absent.

### 2.2 Complex contagion vs. simple contagion

- **Centola, D. & Macy, M. 2007** "Complex Contagions and the Weakness of Long Ties" *AJS* 113(3):702–734. The key distinction. Not in bib. [`centola2007`]
- **Centola, D. 2018** *How Behavior Spreads: The Science of Complex Contagions*. Princeton UP. [`centola2018`]
- **Centola et al. 2018** "Experimental evidence for tipping points in social convention" *Science* 360(6393):1116–1119. Empirical test of tipping-point prediction. [`centola2018b`]
- **Guilbeault & Centola 2021** "Topological measures for identifying and predicting the spread of complex contagions" *Nature Communications* 12. [`guilbeault2021`]
- **Peralta, Kertész & Iñiguez 2024** "Emergence of simple and complex contagion dynamics from weighted belief networks" *Science Advances* 10. Models how simple/complex dichotomy emerges from belief-network weights. Directly relevant to the chapter's attempt to unify SIR and threshold dynamics. [`peralta2024`]

Recommendation: the SIR section (§11.2) assumes simple contagion (one-contact transmission). Complex-contagion dynamics are different — they require multiple reinforcement sources. A short paragraph in §11.2 or §11.6 acknowledging this would strengthen the chapter's taxonomy.

### 2.3 Belief dynamics with bounded or naive agents

- **Acemoglu, Chernozhukov & Yildiz 2016** "Fragility of asymptotic agreement under Bayesian learning" *Theoretical Economics* 11(1):187–225. Shows that Bayesian agents can fail to converge when they disagree about the likelihoods of different signals, not just priors. Directly relevant to the chapter's Theorem 11.1 on polarization. [`acemoglu2016`]
- **Jadbabaie et al. 2012** "Non-Bayesian social learning" *Games and Economic Behavior* 76(1):210–225. Bridge between DeGroot and Bayesian models. [`jadbabaie2012`]
- **Mostagir & Siderius 2022** "Learning in a post-truth world" *Management Science*. A very recent model of Bayesian agents in polarizing media environments. [`mostagir2022` — verify]

### 2.4 Network science for belief diffusion

- **Pastor-Satorras et al. 2015** "Epidemic processes in complex networks" *Reviews of Modern Physics* 87(3):925–979. The canonical review for SIR on networks. [`pastorSatorras2015`]
- **Watts 2002** "A simple model of global cascades on random networks" *PNAS* 99(9):5766–5771. Already cited as `watts2002`. Good.
- **Lazer et al. 2020** "Computational social science: Obstacles and opportunities" *Science* 369(6507):1060–1062. Update to the 2009 Lazer et al. (verify if earlier Lazer is in bib). [`lazer2020`]

### 2.5 Agent-based models in social science

- **Epstein 2006** *Generative Social Science: Studies in Agent-Based Computational Modeling*. Princeton UP. [`epstein2006`]
- **Salganik 2018** *Bit by Bit: Social Research in the Digital Age*. Princeton UP. Already cited. Confirm bib key `salganik2018`.
- **Axelrod 1997** *The Complexity of Cooperation: Agent-Based Models of Competition and Collaboration*. Princeton UP. Not in bib. [`axelrod1997`]


## 3. Non-English sources

Ch. 11 is formal. Mathematical-modeling literatures in French, Italian, German, and Russian exist but citations are less forced by disciplinary norms in this subfield than in sociology or humanities. The book's Anglo-centrism in this chapter is less acute than in Ch. 1 or 25. That said:

- **Deffuant, G. et al. 2000** "Mixing beliefs among interacting agents" *Advances in Complex Systems* 3:87–98. A French-led team's contribution to the bounded-confidence lineage; alternative to Hegselmann-Krause. [`deffuant2000`]
- Russian mathematical-sociology tradition (Mikhailov, Gubanov et al.) on opinion-dynamics — a gap if the chapter wants full-spectrum coverage.

## 5. Proposed prose revisions

1. **§11.6 Model Comparison table (L430–442)**: add a sixth row for **Opinion Dynamics** (continuous-state, DeGroot/HK). The current five families omit the continuous-state averaging tradition, which is arguably the largest living literature on belief dynamics. Alternatively, name it in §"Notes and References" with one sentence.
2. **§11.2 SIR model**: add a one-sentence caveat that SIR assumes simple contagion; for many belief dynamics, multiple reinforcement sources are required (complex contagion, Centola 2007, 2018). This does not require restructuring the chapter, just an acknowledging footnote.
3. **§11.3 Bayesian updating, Theorem 11.1 (L231)**: add a "cf." note citing Acemoglu, Chernozhukov & Yildiz 2016 on the fragility of agreement under Bayesian learning. The theorem in the book is for fixed likelihood; A-C-Y shows what happens when likelihoods themselves are in dispute.
4. **§11.5 Lotka-Volterra**: the framing "belief competition" would be strengthened by citing Cavalli-Sforza & Feldman 1981 or one of the Boyd-Richerson cultural-evolution works. The current `nowak2006` citation is about cooperation, not competition.
5. **§"Notes and References" (L456–464)**: convert all `\textcite`s from the prose-mentioned citations (Sterman 2000, Pastor-Satorras 2015, Chamley 2004, Epstein 2006, Glaeser & Sunstein 2009) into `\autocite`s after the relevant bib entries are added. Currently these are name-dropped without `\cite`.


## 6. Open questions specific to Ch. 11

1. **Parameter calibration for the five model families.** The chapter's opening warning (L14–16) acknowledges that calibration is a major open problem. A concrete calibration for at least one canonical belief (e.g., the selfishness regime of Ch. 21) using real panel data would be a paper-length project that could validate the framework.
2. **The relationship between the five families.** Are they disjoint? Complementary? Hierarchically related? The chapter's Model-Selection principle (L444) is practical but theoretical clarity on the taxonomy would strengthen the chapter.
3. **Extension to continuous-state models.** Opinion-dynamics models (DeGroot / HK) are the continuous-state analogue of SIR. A subsection or sister chapter would close a gap.
4. **Empirical tests of the bifurcation prediction.** Centola 2018 *Science* provides one empirical test of the tipping-point prediction. The chapter's Proposition 11.2 (funding bifurcation) has no direct empirical test. The Piatra Institute playgrounds mentioned in §"ABMs and the Playgrounds" could run the relevant simulations; an empirical test in a real population is an open problem.
5. **Polarization beyond the two-signal-source model.** The theorem's two-group setup is clean. Multi-group polarization, information-network structure, and endogenous channel selection (agents choose what they're exposed to) are all live research areas. A short paragraph pointing to these extensions would future-proof the chapter.
