**Thesis:** Doxonomics is the study of how material power shapes public belief. Its central research question is not whether a belief is true, but what it costs to make it appear self-evident.
**Companion:** `docs/research/book.md` (field-level landscape).

Ch. 1 introduces the field. It is the book's definition chapter: the belief production chain, the conversion problem, the first-order belief equation, the scope claim, the neighboring-fields table. Much of the chapter is stipulative (definitions, scope delimiters); a smaller set of empirical claims anchors the motivation.

## 2. Specific primary-source upgrades available

The chapter already cites the right secondary-literature anchors. The following are primary-source additions that would strengthen specific claims.

### 2.1 Bourdieu's *doxa* (claim C11)

Currently the chapter references doxa via its usage throughout the Bourdieu corpus (`bourdieu1984`, `bourdieu1991`). The primary formulation is in *Outline of a Theory of Practice* (Cambridge UP English translation 1977 of the French 1972 original). The pages that matter are Bourdieu 1977 pp. 164–170.

**Proposed addition:**
- Bourdieu, P. 1977. *Outline of a Theory of Practice*. Trans. R. Nice. Cambridge UP. [`bourdieu1977`]

### 2.2 Henrich and the conditional-cooperation evidence base

Chapter 1 references "humans are conditional cooperators" in L128 without citation, and Ch. 21 carries the full citation stack (`fischbacher2001`, `henrich2001`, `bowles2011`). The cross-reference from L127 to Ch. 21 is implicit. Consider:

- Adding a footnote at L128 pointing explicitly to `\ref{ch:human-nature}` and adding the Henrich 2020 book as an additional reference.

**Proposed addition:**
- Henrich, J. 2020. *The WEIRDest People in the World*. FSG. [`henrich2020`]
- Henrich, Heine & Norenzayan 2010 "The weirdest people in the world?" *BBS* 33(2-3):61–83. [`henrich2010`]

### 2.3 Ostrom as live counter-example

The Ostrom 1990 citation in Ch. 21 (`ostrom1990`) could be forward-referenced from Ch. 1 §"The Belief Production Chain" to strengthen the chapter's claim that counter-evidence exists. One sentence: "the experimental and field evidence against pure self-interest is developed in Ch. 21; Ostrom 1990 collected the commons-governance evidence into the most-cited challenge to the tragedy-of-the-commons prediction."

### 2.4 The conversion problem's intellectual prehistory

Ch. 1 L99 frames "How are resources converted into durable public belief?" as the foundational question. Implicit prehistory:

- Bernays 1928 *Propaganda* (already in bib as `bernays1928`) on the engineering of consent.
- Lippmann 1922 *Public Opinion* (already in bib as `lippmann1922`).
- Harold Lasswell's 1927 *Propaganda Technique in the World War* — **likely not in bib**. A canonical precursor to Herman-Chomsky. [T1, HIGH, P2]

**Proposed addition:**
- Lasswell, H.D. 1927. *Propaganda Technique in the World War*. Knopf. [`lasswell1927`]

## 7. Open questions specific to Ch. 1

1. **The claim that no existing discipline tracks doxonomic flows "as a unified phenomenon" (L27).** This is the central field-founding claim. The book's case is partly survey (§"Neighboring Fields") and partly definitional (the accounting commitment). A short footnote acknowledging that some fields *do* track adjacent unified flows — e.g., computational social science efforts to map information-cascade dynamics across platforms — would strengthen the claim by conceding the adjacent cases while preserving the distinctive commitment.
2. **Is the first-order belief equation meant to be testable?** L119 says "not a mature model" and L120 lists its simplifications. A sentence on what a maturation would look like (panel data on $F$, $C$, $P$, $T$ for a specific belief over time) would point toward the Ch. 13–14 methods.
3. **The "not conspiracy theory" disclaimer (L225).** The argument is good. It could be strengthened by a forward reference to specific mechanisms the book later offers (e.g., the five Herman-Chomsky filters as a structural-bias mechanism that does not require coordination).
